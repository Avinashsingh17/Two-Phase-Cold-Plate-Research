## Overview

Professor Issam Mudawar leads the Purdue University Boiling and Two-Phase Flow Laboratory (PU-BTPFL, later PUREES — Purdue University Research and Education Enterprise for the Study of Boiling). The lab has produced the most extensive body of work on critical heat flux and flow boiling in channels, spanning subcooled through saturated regimes, water and dielectric fluids, macro- and micro-channels, and Earth and microgravity conditions. Their data and correlations are the de facto benchmarks for CHF and boiling heat transfer in the thermal management community.

## Key Contributions

- Compiled the PU-BTPFL CHF database (5544 subcooled data points) — the largest open subcooled CHF database — and developed the Hall & Mudawar inlet-conditions correlation (MAE 10.3%), the most accurate subcooled CHF correlation for water in round tubes [Hall & Mudawar 2000].
- Extensive experimental campaigns on flow boiling in microchannels and mini-channels, including rectangular channels relevant to cold plate geometries [Qu & Mudawar 2003, and others].
- Systematic parametric studies on ultra-high CHF at high mass velocities and small diameters [Mudawar & Bowers 1999].
